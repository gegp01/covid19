Covid-19 pandemic causes human distress, death
and economic crisis to all world nations.



After a year of the first infected patient in Wuhan China, 137 million people were infected with coronavirus SARS-CoV-2 and almost 3 million died of Covid-19.



Can we predict the risks of Covid-19?



The epidemiological intelligence platform EpIPUMA generates predictions trained on historical data of thousands different conditions.

MODEL (profiling)

Predictive models (profiling)

Profiling models can predict the districts (municipios) without Covid-19 cases that may present them, because they resemble districts with Covid-19 in the training period (profile).

Map prediction: Districts in blue are the most likely to have Covid-19 cases. In this example, predictions are based on 15 days of training data on demography and mobility. Districts in red confirm the prediction. Precision is the ratio: confirmed / predicted, and increases from the beginning of the time period as the information acumulates.

EpIPUMA learns and evaluates causal relationships by considering all available information, for example demography, mobility and vulnerability.

MODEL (Ensemble of people)

Predictive models (ensemble)

EpIPUMA predicts the personal risk of dying based on vulnerability (age group) and the risk of Covid-19 in the district of residence.

Personal vulnerability and exposure to Covid-19 are combined in the ensemble model to predict the number of people that is likely to die in a district.

Map: Accuracy is here represented as the districts predicted with the highest number of dead people (blue), confirmed by the number of dead people observed in that district (red).

Machine learning

Machine learning


Complex systems like the Covid-19 pandemic are adaptive, hence machine learning is a fundamental feature of EpIPUMA.


World cloud: Risk factors associated with detecting Covid-19 cases in districts with no cases. Note that risk factos change at every period of time.
