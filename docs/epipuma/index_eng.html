EpIPUMA
Epidemiological Intelligence Platform


Covid-19 pandemic causes human pain, death
and big economical losses in all world nations.

The disease is caused by infection with coronavirus SARS CoV-2.
After a year of the first infected patient in Wuhan China,
137 million people were infected with the pathogen and almost 3 million died.


Can we predict the risks of Covid-19?

The epidemiological intelligence platform EpIPUMA
generates predictions trained on historical data
on thousands different conditions (factors).

MODEL (profiling)

Predictive models (profiling)

EpIPUMA learns and evaluates causal relationships by considering all available information, for example demography, mobility and vulnerability.

Profiling models can predict the districts (municipios) without Covid-19 cases that may present them, because they resemble districts with Covid-19 in the training period (profile).

Map prediction: Districts in blue are the most likely to have Covid-19 cases. In this example, predictions are based on 15 days of training data on demography and mobility. Districts in red confirm the prediction. Precision is the ratio: confirmed / predicted, and increases from the beginning of the time period as the information acumulates.
MODEL (Ensemble of people)

Predictive models (ensemble)

EpIPUMA predicts the personal risk of dying based on vulnerability (age group) and the risk of Covid-19 in the district of residence. Persons’ vulnerability and exposure to Covid-19 are combined in the ensemble model to predict the probability of death. This likelihood is represented as a Bayesian score.

Map: Red districts indicate a high risk of dying from Covid-19. R² captures the fit between the predicted and the observed deaths.
Machine learning

Machine learning


Complex systems like the Covid-19 pandemic are adaptive, hence machine learning is a fundamental feature of EpIPUMA.
